GDP per capita and Suicide rate

In the recent years, there has been an increasing number of suicides among the youths. One main reason highlighted is poor standards of living or lack of money. The below visualisations tries to explore the concern. In the analysis, GDP per capita can be taken as a rough estimate for the standard of living and a normalised value for suicide rates among 100,000 people can be taken.

Top and Bottom 3 GDP per capita countries and their Suicide rates

The below graph aim to compare the GDP per capita of the top and bottom 3 countries namely Luxembourg, Switzerland, Norway, Togo, Uganda and Chad and understand their relationship with suicide rates. The initial prediction is that countries with higher GDP per capita will be having lower suicide rates because a higher GDP per capita means higher average income per household. Low income is usually one of the major reasons of suicide because lack of money in the long term leader to poverty, hunger and poor standards of living

The finding from the graph above are rather controversial where it has been discovered that the top 3 countires based on GDP per capita have higher suicide rates as compared to the bottom 3 countires based on GDP per Capita. The results show that Switzerland has the highest suicide rate of 17.1 and 2nd highest GDP of US($) 82,082 per capita in 2015 However, on the other hand Chad has the lowest suicide rate of 8.9 and 3rd lowest GDP per capita of US($) 776 in 2015. From the analysis of this data it is evident that increase in GDP per capita is directly proportional to suicide rates. One reason for this may be social media, with high GDP per capita countries more and more youth have access to social media which results in open access to such public forums which if not used appropriately is a source of cyber abuse.
